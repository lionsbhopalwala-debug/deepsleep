The Vampire Hormone

Melatonin is often called the "Vampire Hormone" because it only emerges in total darkness. Its job isn't to knock you out like a sedative, but to signal every cell in your body that the rest-and-repair window has opened.

Most people fail because they treat melatonin as a pill. Real bio-hacking is about managing the dim-light melatonin onset (DLMO). If you miss this window, your body stays in a state of high alertness, ruining your 90-minute sleep architecture.

The Supplement Trap

Taking high doses (5mg-10mg) of synthetic melatonin can actually desensitize your receptors. The goal is to encourage endogenous production. Micro-dosing (0.3mg) is often more effective for resetting the clock than massive doses that cause morning grogginess.

Melatonin FAQ

1. What is the best time to take melatonin? ↓

If using a supplement for jet lag, take it 30-60 minutes before your target bedtime. For natural production, aim for total darkness 2 hours before sleep.

2. Can melatonin cause vivid dreams? ↓

Yes. High doses can increase REM intensity, leading to very vivid or even lucid dreams, but this can sometimes fragment your deep sleep stages.

3. Why do I wake up at 3 AM after taking it? ↓

This is often a "rebound effect" or a sign of high Cortisol. Your body processes the melatonin, and then the stress hormone takes over too early.

4. Does screen light destroy melatonin instantly? ↓

Not instantly, but it halts the rate of secretion. Every minute of scrolling is a minute your brain thinks it's daytime.

5. Is it safe for long-term use? ↓

Long-term use can suppress your body's natural ability to produce it. It's best used as a reset tool rather than a nightly crutch.
