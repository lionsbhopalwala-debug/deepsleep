Mineral FAQ

1. Why is Magnesium Glycinate better than Oxide? ↓

Magnesium Oxide has an absorption rate of only ~4%, meaning it mostly passes through your gut. **Glycinate** is chelated with Glycine, making it highly bioavailable and gentle on the stomach.

2. Can I take too much Magnesium? ↓

The upper limit for supplemental magnesium is generally 350mg-400mg. Excessive doses can lead to diarrhea or lethargy. Always consult a physician for personalized dosing.

3. Does Magnesium cross the blood-brain barrier? ↓

Most forms do not efficiently. Only **Magnesium L-Threonate** has been scientifically shown to effectively cross the blood-brain barrier, specifically targeting synapse density and cognitive health.

4. Should I take it with or without food? ↓

Magnesium can be taken with food to minimize stomach upset, but for sleep purposes, taking it on an empty stomach 1 hour before bed allows for faster GABAergic activation.

5. What are the signs of Magnesium deficiency? ↓

Muscle cramps, eyelid twitches, high anxiety, and "Restless Leg Syndrome" are common indicators that your magnesium stores are depleted.
