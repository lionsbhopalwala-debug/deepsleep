The Adenosine Blockade

Throughout your waking hours, a molecule called Adenosine builds up in your brain. This is your "Sleep Pressure." By the time you reach 16 hours of wakefulness, adenosine levels are high enough to trigger the 90-minute sleep cycle cascade.

Caffeine is a master deceiver. It is structurally similar to adenosine and effectively plugs the adenosine receptors in your brain. It doesn’t create energy; it merely hides the exhaustion. Once the caffeine is metabolized, the "Adenosine Flood" begins, causing the notorious Caffeine Crash.

The Brutal Math of Half-Life

For most humans, caffeine has a half-life of approximately 5 to 6 hours. This means if you consume 200mg (a double espresso) at 4:00 PM, by 10:00 PM, you still have 100mg active in your bloodstream. This is enough to delay the onset of Deep Sleep by up to 40%.

Caffeine FAQ

1. How long before bed should I stop caffeine? ↓

For a perfect 90-minute cycle, the "Caffeine Cut-off" should be at least 10 hours before sleep. If you sleep at 11 PM, stop by 1 PM.

2. Why does coffee make some people sleepy? ↓

This is often due to extreme dehydration or the "Adenosine Flood" happening faster than the caffeine can plug the receptors. It’s a sign of severe sleep debt.

3. Does darker roast have more caffeine? ↓

Contrary to belief, Light Roasts often have slightly more caffeine. Roasting burns off caffeine; dark roasts just taste stronger but are metabolically lighter.

4. Can food slow down caffeine absorption? ↓

Yes. Consuming caffeine on an empty stomach leads to a massive plasma spike. Consuming it with fats (like Bulletproof coffee) slows down the absorption rate.

5. What is the fastest way to metabolize caffeine? ↓

Exercise, intense hydration, and Magnesium intake help the liver process enzymes faster, reducing the jitters and clearing the system for night.
