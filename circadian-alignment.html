Every cell in your body has a clock, but they all take orders from the **Master Clock**—the Suprachiasmatic Nucleus (SCN) located in the hypothalamus. **Circadian Alignment** is the process of ensuring this master clock is perfectly synced with the external world's light-dark cycle.

When you are "misaligned," you experience a biological friction known as **Social Jetlag**. This happens when your internal biology is at 3:00 AM, but your alarm clock says it is 7:00 AM. The result is systemic inflammation, brain fog, and a total collapse of the 90-minute sleep cycle.

Alignment FAQ

1. Can I use a SAD lamp instead of real sunlight? ↓

Yes, a 10,000 lux light box is a valid "Zeitgeber" if you live in a low-sunlight environment. However, real photons from the sun are always superior for full spectrum synchronization.

2. Does eating late at night break alignment? ↓

Yes. Your liver and digestive system have their own circadian clocks. Eating late sends a "daytime" signal to your gut while your brain thinks it is "nighttime," causing **Metabolic Dissonance**.

3. What is "Social Jetlag"? ↓

Social Jetlag is the discrepancy between your biological clock and your social obligations (work/school). It is the leading cause of chronic fatigue in the modern world.

4. Does temperature affect the SCN? ↓

Temperature is a powerful secondary Zeitgeber. A cold environment at night and a warm core temperature during the day help reinforce the circadian signals sent by the brain.

5. How do I fix my rhythm if I work night shifts? ↓

Shift workers must use "Artificial Phase Shifting." Use high-intensity blue light during your shift and wear dark red-tinted glasses the moment you finish work to trick the SCN into thinking it is night.
